Sewon Min is a Ph.D. candidate in the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering at the University of Washington, advised by Prof. Luke Zettlemoyer and Prof. Hannaneh Hajishirzi. Her research is in the area of natural language processing and machine learning. She is mainly interested in language modeling, retrieval, and their intersection and applications. She was a co-instructor and a co-organizer of multiple tutorials and workshops at ACL, EMNLP, NAACL, NeurIPS and AKBC, including the Tutorial on Zero-/Few-Shot NLP with Pretrained Language Models, Tutorial on Retrieval-based Language Models, The 7th Workshop on Representation Learning for NLP, and Workshop on Semiparametric Methods in NLP. Prior to UW, she obtained a B.S. degree in Computer Science & Engineering from Seoul National University. During Ph.D., she also has been a part-time visiting researcher at Meta AI for four years.
